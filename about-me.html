About Me

Personal Beliefs

Philosophy — Time & Fate — I live by two guiding principles: Que Sera Sera — what will be, will be — a belief that life unfolds as it must, and that resisting the inevitable wastes the energy better spent on growth. But equally, I believe time itself is the one force capable of changing fate. Nothing is permanently fixed. Given enough time, discipline, and intention, even a predetermined outcome can be rewritten. Between acceptance and action, that is where I choose to stand.

Hobbies

Life Beyond the Screen — When the editor is closed, the controller comes out. Gaming is a serious passion — with a catalogue that spans Halo: TMCC, nearly every title in the Call of Duty franchise, and Last of Us. And when the controller is down, the headphones go on. Country music is the soundtrack of choice — a genre built on raw honesty, strong narratives, and emotion that hits differently. Two hobbies that seem unrelated to code, but both speak the same language: story, detail, and craft.

Current Focus

Currently building Chronos v2 — actively developing a full graphical interface for Chronos, my Python-powered Windows voice assistant, so users can select custom voices, define their own commands, and interact without touching the terminal. Alongside that, completing JavaScript to sharpen my frontend skills and bring richer interactivity to my web projects.

Personal Interest

A Developer by Identity — Currently working across Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, each language serving a distinct purpose. HTML, CSS, and JavaScript work to build modern, interactive web experiences — this very website being proof of that. Python operates in powering Chronos, an assistant controls a full Windows system by voice alone. Four languages, two domains.

Goals and Ambitions

Ambition Without a Ceiling — The goal is not just to be good at software development — it is to excel at it, to become the kind of developer who commands any room and solves any problem. That means going beyond the comfortable and learning more than six programming languages across different paradigms — web, systems, scripting, and beyond. But the vision stretches further than a screen. Adding skills in electrical transmission and power systems is part of a larger blueprint: to become a rare kind of engineer who understands both the software that controls the world and the physical infrastructure that powers it. Versatility is the ultimate skill.

Location

Rooted in Kampala, Eyes on the World — A proud Ugandan, born in Mityana and raised in the heart of Kampala — a city that sharpens you, humbles you, and builds character in ways few places can. But ambition doesn't stay still. The goal is to take everything built here — the skills, the drive, the perspective — and carry it abroad, not to leave Uganda behind, but to grow beyond the limits of geography and bring something greater back.
