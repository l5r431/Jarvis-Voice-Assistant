Process Flow

How It Works

A streamlined pipeline from voice to action — all running on your machine.

01
You Speak the Command
Chronos runs quietly in the background, listening via your microphone. When you say the wake phrase followed by an app name, it captures your voice input as a local audio buffer — nothing is streamed anywhere.
02
Vosk Decodes Your Speech
The Vosk offline speech recognition model processes the audio entirely on your CPU. It converts your voice to text in milliseconds using a compact, locally-stored language model trained specifically for command-style speech.
03
Intent Matching
The transcribed text is matched against Chronos's internal command registry, which maps natural-language phrases to your installed applications. Fuzzy matching ensures you don't need to say the exact app name every time.
04
App Launches Instantly
Once a match is found, Chronos fires the corresponding Windows process or shell command. The target application opens — no mouse click, no keyboard shortcut, no context switching required.
05
Chronos Confirms via Voice
Microsoft Edge TTS synthesises a spoken confirmation using the Jarvis-matched voice profile. You hear "Opening Chrome" or "Launching Spotify" — crisp, cool, and immediate. The whole cycle completes in under one second.

Security & Privacy

Your Voice, Your Machine

Privacy is not an optional feature in Chronos — it's the entire architecture.

⚔ Zero Surveillance Architecture

Chronos was designed from day one around a single principle: your voice data is yours alone. There are no analytics, no telemetry, no usage reporting, and absolutely no transmission of audio or transcripts to any server — ever.

  • All speech-to-text runs on your CPU via Vosk
  • No microphone stream sent to the internet
  • No account, login, or registration required
  • Models stored and run 100% locally
  • Voice synthesis via local Kokoro TTS engine
  • No background data collection of any kind
  • Works fully offline after first-use model download
  • Open command logs only on your own device
